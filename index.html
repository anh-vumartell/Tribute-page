Ada Lovelace

One of the first female computer programmers in human history

A portrait of Ada Lovelace

  • 1815 - Born in London, England
  • 1829 - Was paralyzed after a bout of measles
  • 1831 - Was able to walk with crutches. Despites her illnesses, she developed her mathematical and technological skill
  • 1833 - Met and became friend with fellow British mathematician Charles Babbage, who is known as "the father of computers"
  • 1835 - Was married to Willam, 8th Baron King and became Lady King
  • 1842-1843 - Translated an article by Italian military engineer Luigi Menabrea on the calculating engine, supplementing with her own notes which contained an algorithm to be carried out by the machine. a.k.a "Note G". Based on this work, Lovelace is widely considered to be the first computer programmer
  • 1852 - Died of uterine cancer, at the age of 36
  • 1950 - Her notes on Babbage's Analytical Engine were republished as an appendix to B.V.Bowden's Faster than Thought: A Symposium on Digital Computing Machines
